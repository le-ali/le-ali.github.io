Intro

People who have never heard of growth mindset probably imagine that everyone's intelligence is fixed. We are either born smart, dumb, or average, and our ability to learn is pre-determined. We don't question this, and somehow, we've been led to believe that after a certain point we're either smart or not and that is the end of it.

I can still remember clearly that when I was at school sometimes I could hear the teacher say that this person or that person is pretty clever kid. When a “not so clever” kid went up front to ask a question, the teacher had no smile on her face. When the clever one went up front to ask a question, she showed the opposite. This affected me a lot. For the whole time, I truly believed that we are born smart, dumb or just average.

The truth is that intelligence can be developed, just as any physical ability can be developed. It requires work and can be difficult, but it is achievable. Now that I think about it, the problem is not the child who “isn’t clever,” but the teacher that fails to see the child’s intelligence and talents. Their judgments are often based on the tests or assessments that do not consider individual character, health, environmental conditions, and most importantly, neuroplasticity!

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity, also known as neural plasticity or brain plasticity, is the brain’s ability to reorganise and restructure itself on a cellular level. Our brain is full of cells called neurons. At one end of each neuron there are branches called dendrites. The spots where one neuron’s dendrites touch the neighbouring neuron are called synapses. When we learn, the number and strength of then synapses grow. When something is difficult and challenging, putting in added effort builds more dendrites!

Understanding this can bring several benefits to people in different aspects of their lives. It can help people approach learning with a growth mindset. It plays a crucial role in the recovery process after brain injuries or strokes. Understanding these principles can empower individuals to take an active role in their rehabilitation. Recognising the brain’s plasticity can have a positive impact on mental health. It tells us that our brain is not fixed and can change. We can learn to form a new way of thinking in order to improve our mental health.

There are several ways to increase my neuroplasticity:

  • Doing the course with DevAcademy has a significant impact on promoting neuroplasticity. I am learning new things and facing challenges everyday!
  • Expanding my English vocabulary or getting better in English in general.
  • Using my non-dominant hand – I am an ambidextrous and already practice that everyday in my life.
  • Creating artwork – I love painting, especially with watercolour!
  • Reading
  • Playing piano – it is therapy for me!
  • Doing mindfulness meditation

Growth mindset

Growth mindset is based on the belief that your basic qualities are things you can cultivate through your efforts. People with a growth mindset believe that we all have the capacity to learn and grow our skills. Failure is just a valuable lesson, and it is not the grand finale. People who are good at something are so because they built that ability, and we are all in control of our own abilities. I think it is such an important thing to know, because it does change the way we think. It helps us to be more positive towards learning, facing challenges, difficulties and even failure.

I am so glad that I discovered it. I always believed that people’s intelligence and talents have a ceiling. We are born smart, dumb or just average. I find that the people around me further reinforce my existing beliefs, making me believe this even more strongly. Because of that, up until now I have pretty much done everything based on a fixed mindset. Learning that we should have a growth mindset instead of a fixed mindset has given me hope, because I now know my skills, talents and intelligence are not set in stone. We all have the ability to change.

It will take time for me to adjust my thinking patterns, but I will work to incorporate a growth mindset into my daily life. I have to learn to embrace challenges and truly believe that with effort, I can master whatever that I am doing. Failures are just an opportunity to learn and grow.
