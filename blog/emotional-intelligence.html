What is EQ?

Emotional intelligence describes the ability, capacity, skill, or self-perceived ability to identify, assess, and manage the emotions of one’s self, of others, and of groups.

The difference between EQ and IQ?

Intelligence Quotient (IQ) is a measure of a person's cognitive abilities allowing one to acquire knowledge, learn and solve problems while Emotional Quotient (EQ),on the other hand, measures one's ability to monitor emotions, cope with pressures and demands and control one's thoughts and actions.

Why EQ is important?

Self-awareness

EQ helps individuals develop self-awareness, enabling them to recognise their emotions and their effects. It helps people to know their strengths and limits, and sureness about their self-worth and capabilities.

Self-regulation

EQ helps individuals to manage disruptive emotions and impulses. It helps people to maintain standards of honesty and integrity. It enables people to take responsibility for personal performance and increase their flexibility in handling change.

Social awareness

People who have higher EQ can have a better understanding of other people’s feelings and perspective, and they take an active interest in their concerns. They can sense what others need in order to develop, and bolster their abilities.

Social skills

EQ helps people develop better social skills. In the workplace, a leader who has a high EQ can understand and manage their own emotions, which helps them make rational decisions, motivate their team members and inspire others. They can create an environment of trust, empathy and cooperation within their organisations.

Overall well-being

EQ positively impacts an individual’s overall well-being. People with high EQ are more in tune with their emotions, leading to improved selfceare and stress management. Overall they just have an optimal state of being!
