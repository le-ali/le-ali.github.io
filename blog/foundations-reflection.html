  • I have fixed mindset and I need to work on changing it.
  • I have already been learning a lot like piano and watercolor painting, which is building neuroplasticity, but I just didn’t know.
  • Before I thought IQ was most important, but now I realise how important EQ is – in the workplace EQ will help me to interact with others well, but will also help me regulate my emotions and keep me mentally strong.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

In programming, having a clear set of values helps developers make ethical choices, prioritize tasks, and create software that aligns with their principles. Empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings of others. It enables developers to create user-friendly experiences and collaborate effectively in teams. Self-awareness involves understanding one's own emotions, strengths, weaknesses. It helps developers recognise their limitations, seed continuous improvement, and manage their interactions with others. All of these apply to learning too.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

By doing the core learning, I realised that there are still things about myself that I acturally don't know or don't know that well.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

For me, the most challenging aspects of the core learning is to actually implement what I’ve learned into practice. I had a fixed mindset before I started the course, I don’t really like facing challenges, I only do things in my comfort zone. I like to challenge myself only when I feel comfortable with what I’m doing. The intensity of the course and the knowledge that we have to learn is high so my fixed mindset is constantly kicking in. I have to be really mindful of what I am thinking and readjust quickly.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because web development is not solely about technical skills. It also involves effective communication, collaboration and problem-solving with clients and team members. All these aspects require us to really know ourselves and develop better human skills.

By learning neuroplasticity and growth mindset, the knowledge that we’ve gained can motivate us to persist in our learning journey and help us overcome challenges and difficulties. A growth mindset is the belief that abilities and intelligence can be developed through dedication, effort, and learning from mistakes. By learning this, we will be able to face challenges, persist in the face of setbacks, and view failures as opportunities for growth.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Personally, I don’t think studying core learning is a waste of time. Using the time to practise coding is important – after all, that is the skill we want to gain in order to become a software developer – but core learning can help us explore ourselves more and understand ourselves better. For me, by learning neuroplasticity and growth mindset made me realise that our IQ isn’t innate, and it actually can develop. That helps when I face challenges and difficulties. Instead of having self-doubt and just claim that I am just not smart enough to this, I can tell myself that everything is new and I am still learning. It takes trial and error to get it right but I can do it in the end. I think overall it will help us to become more well-rounded developers.
