Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision, discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

When I worked in Beijing, I remembered that one time I got overpaid from work by quite a lot. I decided to tell them about it. They were grateful as they didn’t even realise how much they overpaid me. For me, I think being honest is crusial. I have to be honest with things in order to build trust with the company. It was just the right thing to do whether there is any benefit later or not. The amount of money that I got overpaid might be nothing to the company, but if I haven’t returned it, basically the trust would be gone. Now I think about it and I still believe it is the right thing to do. The company might never find out about that or they might. But for me, being honest that leaves me no regrets.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Cultural upbringing

I grew up in China and attended formal education all the way to University. The educational system in China is extremely competitive. If you’re not the best student in the class then some of the teachers don't give you much attention, and also you need pretty good grades to be able to get in to high school. Because of this, I had to work really hard to get good grades in order to get accept by high school then be able to go to university. This influenced me to work very hard and be extremely self disciplined in order to achieve my goal. Even in difficult situations, I have to tell myself to keep pushing and never give up.

People in my life

I have to say that the person who has influenced me tremendously is my granddad. He is the kindest person that I have known. He showed me so much love and care, and that really shaped me who I am as a person. 

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

Because of the intense nature of the educational system in China, hard work and self-regulation is ingrained into how I study. I am a pretty disciplined person, and I like routine. So If I know I have to do something, I will probably do it everyday. I tend to want to know as much as possible about a subject while learning it, which makes my learning a wider experience.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey.

Language – because English is my second language, there will always be times where I don’t understand what someone said or even meant. Sometimes instructions might take me a bit longer to fully understand. I know this will get better with time and experience, but it’s definitely a limitation to work through.

Self-confidence – I need to build more self-confidence. This is definitely one of my limitations, which brings me self doubt. It will lessen my efficiency in terms of learning.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tired at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

When I first started at a job in Beijing, all my manager did was tell me to "read the website" for 2 weeks to “get to know the company.” Within a few days I’d already done it then they wouldn’t give me anything else to do. As a side note, the workplace in China, much like school, can be very competitive and people worry about losing their job. I wanted to start actually doing meaningful work, but they kept sending me back to look at the website… again!! To deal with this, I insisted on being given some real work and eventually they gave me something useful to do. I used the strategy of persistence and showing that I'm not going to give up, that I’m ready.
